Civic InnovatioN in CommunITY (CIN-CITY): safety, policing and trust with young people

Participant Information Sheet (PIS)

This PIS should be read in conjunction with The University of Manchester Privacy Policy for Research Participants.

You are being invited to take part in a research study concerning people's different perception of urban space. The aim of this research is to find and describe temporal trends of three types of perception in a cyclical time period (days / nights, working days / weekends, seasons). We will study, and subsequently compare, two cities – Manchester (UK) and Olomouc (Czech Republic). Before you decide whether to take part, it is important for you to understand why the research is being conducted and what it will involve. Please take time to read the following information carefully before deciding whether to take part, and discuss it with others if you wish. Please ask if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information. Thank you for taking the time to read this.

Who will conduct the research?

  • Dr. Jonathan Huck – Department of Geography, School of Environment, Education and Development, The University of Manchester
  • Mgr. Lucia Brisudová – Department of Geography, Faculty of Science, Palacký University Olomouc

What is the purpose of the research?

In this research we will focus on three types of perception of space. Places considered as pleasant (1. topophilic), unpleasant (2. topophobic) and abandoned or vacant places that could be treated and utilised in a better way (3. topovacant). We will observe time as an essential component of perception of space. We will collect data on perception using a mobile application which enables real-time and in-situ data collection. This novel approach has the potential to provide new perspectives on the perception of place and therefore could represent an important contribution into behavioural geography. The selection of participants is based on a few pragmatic criteria that include:

  • presence of the participant in the city during whole year,
  • the minimum age (18 years),
  • ownership of a smartphone with an Android operating system.

Last but not least, an interest in the topic of the research is another very important criterium. The higher the number of research participants, the better the quality of the data.

Will the outcomes of the research be published?

The results stemming from this research will be in the form of articles presented in relevant journals (from the field of geography). The findings will also be used in the thesis of the PhD student from Palacký University, Olomouc (Lucia Brisudová).

Who has reviewed the research project?

The project has been reviewed by The University of Manchester Research Ethics Committee.

What would I be asked to do if I took part?

When you take part in the research, you take part in the following activities:

Map-Me online survey – you will be asked to fill out an online survey which includes a basic personal questionnaire with 10 questions. The second part of this survey contains a map and several additional questions. Both parts together will take approximately 20 minutes to complete.

CIN-CITY mobile application – after filling in the Map-Me survey you will be asked to install the mobile application (CIN-CITY). This app will serve as the main reporting tool and is designed for data collection throughout the remainder of the research project. Your role will be recording your current perception of an urban space during three days (Wednesday, Friday, Saturday). You can make reports any time during the day. When making a new report, the application will save your location and current time and ask a few more questions concerning the type of perception. Because of the complexity of the research, this procedure will be repeated in all four seasons (as seen in the scheme below). Three identical data collection days are selected for each season. The time needed for this part of research will differ based on the number of reports you choose to make during each day.

All steps will be explained in information material and video tutorials that every participant receives before the start of the research project.

Will I be compensated for taking part?

There are no compensation arrangements for the participants. Since there should be no expenses for participants, financial compensation is not necessary.

What happens if I do not want to take part or if I change my mind?

It is up to you to decide whether or not to take part. If you ave questions around whether you should decide to take part or not, you should send an email to one of the researchers so they can provide further information. If you do decide to take part, you will be given this information sheet to keep and will be asked to sign a consent form, provide verbal consent or will be asked to tick a box to confirm consent. If you decide to take part you are still free to withdraw at any time without giving a reason and without detriment to yourself. However, it will not be possible to remove any anonymised data from the project as we will not be able to identify your specific data. This does not affect your data protection rights. If you decide not to take part you do not need to do anything further.

Data Protection and Confidentiality

What information will you collect about me?

In order to participate in this research project we will need to collect information that could identify you, called “personal identifiable information” (PII). Specifically, we will need to collect:

  • Gender
  • Age
  • City region where you live in
  • Current working status
  • Level of education
  • Gross monthly income
  • Way of moving around the city
  • The length of your stay in the city
  • Another important places where you spend time during the day (name of the street)

In the mobile application part of the research you may be asked to take a photo / video of places. This will include photos and video of the environment, not photos or videos of you or the person you are with.

Under what legal basis are you collecting this information?

We are collecting and storing this personal identifiable information in accordance with UK data protection law (GDPR) which protect your rights. These state that we must have a legal basis (specific reason) for collecting your data. For this study, the specific reason is that it is “a public interest task” and “a process necessary for research purposes”.

What are my rights in relation to the information you will collect about me?

You have a number of rights under data protection law regarding your personal information. For example you can request a copy of the information we hold about you. If you would like to know more about your different rights or the way we use your personal information to ensure we follow the law, please consult our Privacy Notice for Research Participants.

Will my participation in the study be confidential and my personal identifiable information be protected?

In accordance with data protection law, The University of Manchester is the Data Controller for this project. This means that we are responsible for making sure your personal information is kept secure, confidential and used only in the way you have been told it will be used. All researchers are trained with this in mind, and your data will be looked after in the following ways.

At the beginning of your participation in the research you will get specific personal identification number. This number will serve for some aspects of the research process, but it will also secure anonymisation of your person and the results of your contribution. Only the research team will have access to the key that links this ID number to your personal information.

Your consent form and contact details will be retained for maximum of 10 years. This information will be safely stored in the data system of The University of Manchester and in data storage of Department of Geography, Palacký University Olomouc and are needed for further results processing in the long term.

With your consent your information will be shared and archived in order to support additional research. Data and information sharing between The University of Manchester and Palacký University Olomouc is necessary as the mutual cooperation represents the core research approach of the whole research.

Basic contact details (email / phone) of participants will also be stored (for the same period as results storage). This is only for case of sharing and presenting of final results, but also in case participants would be interested in repeated collaboration on similar projects in future. The information will only be used for the above purposes and cannot be used to contact you regarding any other matter which is not related with this research.

The photos and videos voluntarily captured and uploaded in the mobile application by participants in the course of research will only be used for research purposes and better interpretation of data. With your consent, these materials could be included in articles in journals, in result presentations and into the dissertation thesis as well.

Please also note that individuals from The University of Manchester or regulatory authorities may need to look at the data collected for this study to make sure the project is being carried out as planned. This may involve looking at identifiable data. All individuals involved in auditing and monitoring the study will have a strict duty of confidentiality to you as a research participant.
